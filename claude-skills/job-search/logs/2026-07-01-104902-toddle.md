## Answers

**Q1: What interests you about working for this company?**

EdTech is one of the few domains where software has a direct, measurable effect on how people learn and teach every day. The fact that Toddle runs in real classrooms means your feedback loop is genuine — teachers and students aren't abstract users, they're people you can actually watch use what you build. That grounding makes both the product instincts and the reliability bar sharper than in most SaaS contexts.

I'm drawn to the backend ownership model here. I've shipped features end-to-end at Credible Informatics and maintained production systems at One2N, but Toddle's pace — moving from fundamentals to owning backend modules within a year — is the kind of trajectory where I know I'll grow fast. The cross-functional setup with educators, QA, and product teams also appeals to me because I think the best backend decisions come from actually understanding why users need something, not just what the ticket says.

The culture values Toddle names — Coach-ability, Curiosity, Hustle, Ownership, Humility — describe an environment where honest feedback flows and people move with urgency without ego. That's where I do my best work.
